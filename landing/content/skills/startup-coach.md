# Startup Coach

This skill provides structured business coaching for solo founders. It walks you through the progression from mission/vision to testable hypotheses, Jobs-to-be-Done analysis, and user story mapping. The goal is to make sure you're building something people actually want before investing months of engineering time.

The agent plays the role of an experienced startup advisor — asking hard questions, challenging assumptions, and keeping you focused on validation over building.

## How It Works

1. **Clarify mission and vision** — Start with why. What problem are you solving? For whom? Why does it matter to you personally? The agent pushes until the answers are crisp and specific.
2. **Frame hypotheses** — Convert your product idea into testable hypotheses: "If we build [solution], then [target users] will [desired outcome]." Each hypothesis needs a clear validation method.
3. **JTBD analysis** — Identify the jobs your users are hiring your product to do. Map the functional, emotional, and social dimensions of each job. This reveals what to build and what to skip.
4. **Story mapping** — Lay out the user journey from first contact to core value delivery. Identify the minimum viable path and the features that can wait.
5. **Validation planning** — Design cheap experiments to test your riskiest assumptions first. Define success criteria before running the experiment.

## When to Use

- Before starting a new project — validate the idea before writing code
- When an existing product isn't gaining traction — diagnose whether the problem is product, market, or distribution
- During quarterly OKR reviews to reassess strategic direction
- When you feel stuck and need an outside perspective on your business

## Requirements

- No technical integrations needed — this is a conversational coaching skill
- Willingness to be challenged on your assumptions
- Access to your OKRs and project strategy docs for context
- Time for an honest, focused conversation (30-60 minutes works well)
