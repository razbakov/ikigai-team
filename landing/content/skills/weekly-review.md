# Weekly Review

The Weekly Review is your strategic checkpoint. Running every Saturday, it zooms out from daily tasks to evaluate whether you're making progress on what actually matters. It covers OKR tracking, sprint retrospective, inbox cleanup, and next-week planning.

Where the daily review asks "what do I do today?", the weekly review asks "am I heading in the right direction?" It catches drift early — before a full quarter passes without meaningful progress on your goals.

## How It Works

1. **Review the week** — Summarize what was accomplished across all projects. Check completed tasks, merged PRs, published content, and meetings held.
2. **Evaluate OKRs** — Compare current progress against quarterly key results. Flag any that are off-track and need intervention.
3. **Sprint retrospective** — What worked well? What caused friction? What should change next week? Capture concrete lessons.
4. **Clean up** — Process any leftover inbox items, close stale tasks, archive completed work, and update project statuses.
5. **Plan next week** — Set top 3 priorities for the week, assign tasks to agents, and identify any commitments or deadlines.

## When to Use

- Every Saturday morning as a dedicated planning block
- At the start or end of a sprint cycle
- Before quarterly OKR reviews to prepare talking points
- When you feel disconnected from your goals and need to recalibrate

## Requirements

- **Notion** — for OKR tracking and task board management
- **GitHub** — to review merged PRs and sprint progress
- Previous daily review files for the week (the agent references them for continuity)
- A quiet block of time — this review benefits from your active participation, not just agent output
