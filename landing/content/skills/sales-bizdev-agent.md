# Sales & Business Development

This skill manages your business development pipeline from lead generation through outreach to deal closure. It researches prospects, identifies good fits, crafts personalized outreach messages, and tracks where every deal stands. For solo founders, this replaces the sales team you can't afford yet.

The agent focuses on quality over quantity. Instead of spray-and-pray outreach, it researches each prospect to find genuine alignment between what you offer and what they need.

## How It Works

1. **Define your ideal customer** — Clarify who you're selling to: industry, company size, role, pain points, and budget range. The tighter the definition, the better the targeting.
2. **Research prospects** — Find potential customers through web research, social media, community directories, and industry events. For each prospect, gather context: what are they working on, what challenges do they face, what have they said publicly.
3. **Qualify leads** — Score each prospect on fit (do they match your ideal customer?) and timing (are they actively looking for a solution?). Focus outreach on high-scoring leads.
4. **Craft outreach** — Write personalized messages that reference the prospect's specific situation. No templates — each message should demonstrate you understand their world.
5. **Track and follow up** — Maintain a pipeline with clear stages (prospect, contacted, meeting scheduled, proposal sent, closed/lost). Follow up consistently without being pushy. Update the CRM after every interaction.

## When to Use

- When you have a product ready for paying customers but no sales process
- To build a pipeline before a product launch
- For partnership outreach where you need to demonstrate mutual value
- During fundraising to identify and approach potential investors
- When organic growth isn't sufficient and you need proactive outreach

## Requirements

- A clear value proposition and pricing (even if rough)
- **Notion** or another CRM to track the pipeline
- Access to prospect research tools (LinkedIn, company websites, social media)
- Templates for outreach messages that you've reviewed and approved
- The discipline to follow up — most deals are won on the 3rd-5th touchpoint, not the first
